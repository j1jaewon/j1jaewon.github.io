# Global Certification Knowledge Base

Accumulated knowledge on compulsory certification requirements across countries,
built through hands-on research for Korean export support work.

## Country Coverage (from ChatGPT conversation history)
- **Southeast Asia**: Vietnam, Malaysia (SIRIM), Indonesia (SNI, HALAL, TKDN)
- **Middle East**: Saudi Arabia, UAE, Egypt
- **Latin America**: Mexico, Ecuador (INEN/RTE INEN), Argentina (INTI), Chile, Brazil
- **Europe**: EU (CE marking, Ecodesign, cosmetics REACH)
- **Eastern Europe/Central Asia**: Ukraine, EAEU (TR CU)
- **East Asia**: China (CCC), Hong Kong

## Key Certification Frameworks

### EU
- **CE Marking**: mandatory for most electronics, machinery, medical devices
- **Ecodesign Regulation**: energy efficiency, repairability
- **REACH/CLP**: chemical substance restrictions (inc. cosmetics)
- Reference: [[bridge-project]] for cosmetics AI verification tool

### Latin America
- **Ecuador**: RTE INEN (technical regulations), NTE INEN (standards), Registro Sanitario
  - Energy efficiency certification applies to home appliances
- **Argentina**: INTI certification, Secretaría de Comercio
- **Mexico**: NOM marks (Normas Oficiales Mexicanas)

### Southeast Asia
- **Malaysia**: SIRIM certification for electronics, telecom equipment
- **Vietnam**: Self-declaration or mandatory certification depending on product;
  similar structure to Korea's 전파법/전안법
- **Indonesia**: SNI (mandatory for many categories), HALAL (mandatory for food/cosmetics),
  TKDN (local content requirement) — see conversation [38] for regulatory analysis strategy

### Middle East
- **Saudi Arabia/UAE**: SASO, ESMA marks; ESS and delivery robots require specific approvals
- **Egypt**: EOS certification

### Standards Reference Work
- Regularly translates and maps international standards:
  IEC, ISO, COPANT, EN, IRAM, NTE INEN, JIS, JS (Jordan)
- Common task: find official standard name (Korean + English) + official URL

## Recurring Research Pattern
1. Identify product + target country
2. Find governing law/regulation (관보, WTO TBT notification)
3. Map to certification scheme (mandatory vs. voluntary, CoC-based vs. type approval)
4. Identify designated CB (conformity body) and testing requirements
5. Summarize for Korean exporter audience

## Tacit Knowledge
- Most countries follow similar structures but use different scheme numbers (Scheme 1/5/7)
- "Approval type" wording in regulations often signals whether self-declaration or CB-issued CoC is required
- HALAL is the #1 regulatory concern for Indonesia — more pervasive than SNI in practice
